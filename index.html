Syntax

The system initialises itself to the first symbol in your ruleset

symbol meaning
F draw forwards by [size]
+ / - rotate clockwise/anticlockwise by [angle]
> / < increase/decrease [size] by 1%
) / ( increase / decrease [angle] by 5%
[ , ] push / pop stack
push saves the current position of the turtle - pop will return the turtle to that state
! flip [angle] through x-axis (kind of. actually it's angle *= -1)
| reverse current direction (adds 180 to angle)

What the hell is this?

Welcome to lsys - the brainchild of a bored programmer with very poor taste in design.

The white pattern you see drawn on your screen is an l-system. An l-system comprises a set of atoms (each of which is assigned some meaning) and a set of production rules which expand individual atoms into sequences of atoms. Some atoms are defined by me - the rules are entirely up to you. You write them in the box on the left, and they take the following form:

[AtomToExpand] : [Sequence of atoms]

The system initialises itself to your first atom, and the follows the production rules you've defined, [levels] times, mutating itself along the way. When expansion has finished, the atoms are then interpreted as a sequence of turtle-graphics instructions. In this stage, only the special characters F,+,-,[,],>,<,| and ! are given any meaning - all others are ignored.
